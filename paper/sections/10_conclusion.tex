\section{Conclusion}

This paper presented a minimal axiomatic theory of deterministic execution and observation without normativity. The theory defines atomic events, an append-only ordered stream, and observation as a non-derivational artifact. Under the axioms, canonical projections are deterministic, observation-free, and replayable. The central design principle is that deterministic replay becomes straightforward when execution structure is separated from observation by construction, rather than by post-hoc filtering. The result is a structural notion of determinism that is independent of outcome meaning or domain semantics.

The theory is intentionally prior to governance, policy, and application semantics. Companion architecture and implementation repositories may build on this model for experimentation and demonstration, but they are not required to understand the theoretical claims made here.
